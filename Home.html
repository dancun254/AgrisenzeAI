Understanding the Problem

Nitrates naturally cycle through the environment fixed in the soil, broken down, and taken up by plants. But human activities‚Äîlike burning fossil fuels and using fertilizers disrupt this natural balance. As a result, nitrates build up in the soil, seep into water sources, and cause eutrophication, which harms aquatic life.

Fertilizers have boosted crop yields, but over-fertilization is a major issue. It harms the environment and damages crops, too. So, how do we keep feeding people while preserving nature? The answer lies in precision farming using just the right amount of fertilizer. To do that, we need to constantly measure nitrates in the soil.
