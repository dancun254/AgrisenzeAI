The Challenge of Over-Fertilization and Water Pollution

Farmers face a constant struggle with over-fertilization, which leads to soil degradation and water pollution. A lack of precise data often forces farmers to guess how much fertilizer is needed. This results in overuse, causing harmful nitrate build-up in the soil and contaminating groundwater and waterways.

The Nitrate Cycle Disruption

Nitrates play a vital role in plant growth, cycling naturally through the environment. They are fixed in the soil, broken down, and taken up by plants. However, human activities such as burning fossil fuels and overuse of fertilizers have disrupted this delicate cycle. When nitrates accumulate, they seep into the water through groundwater and runoff, causing eutrophication.

What is Eutrophication?

Eutrophication occurs when water bodies become overly enriched with nutrients like nitrates, leading to:

  • Excessive algae growth, which blocks sunlight from reaching aquatic life.
  • Oxygen depletion, suffocating fish and other aquatic organisms.

This process doesn't just harm water bodies. Over-fertilization also damages crops, causing lower yields and wasted resources.

Governments across Africa are introducing stricter regulations to curb nitrate pollution, and compliance with these regulations adds extra financial pressure on farmers.
